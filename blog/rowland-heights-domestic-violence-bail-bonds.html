Bail Bonds for Rowland Heights Domestic Violence Arrests — What Families Need to Know

A domestic violence arrest in Rowland Heights can hit a family without warning. One phone call changes everything — and most families in this tight-knit San Gabriel Valley community have never navigated the bail system before. If someone you love was just arrested, this guide will walk you through exactly what happens next, where they're being held, what bail will cost, and how to get them home as fast as possible.

What Happens After a DV Arrest in Rowland Heights

Rowland Heights is an unincorporated community within Los Angeles County, which means the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department — not a local police department — responds to all calls, including domestic violence incidents. When a DV call comes in, Sheriff's deputies from the Industry Sheriff's Station (located at 150 N. Hudson Ave, Industry, CA 91744) are typically dispatched.

Under California law, if deputies observe visible injuries, corroborating physical evidence, or receive consistent statements from the reporting party, they are generally required to make an arrest — even if the alleged victim does not want to press charges. This mandatory arrest policy surprises many Rowland Heights families unfamiliar with how California DV law works.

After arrest, the person is transported to Industry Sheriff's Station for initial booking. This process includes:

  • Photographing and fingerprinting
  • Recording the charges and personal information
  • A background check to determine if warrants or prior DV convictions exist
  • Bail determination based on the LA County bail schedule

For cases involving more serious charges or when Industry Station is at capacity, the arrested person may be transferred to Twin Towers Correctional Facility in Los Angeles for housing until arraignment. Arraignment for Rowland Heights DV cases is typically held at the Governor George Deukmejian Courthouse in Pomona, which serves the East San Gabriel Valley judicial district.

Important: Rowland Heights has no local police department. All law enforcement services — including DV response, arrest, and booking — are handled exclusively by the LA County Sheriff's Department through Industry Station.

LA County Bail Amounts for Domestic Violence Charges

Bail in Rowland Heights DV cases follows the Los Angeles County Felony Bail Schedule. The amounts below represent typical bail set at booking, though a judge may raise or lower bail at arraignment based on the specific facts of the case, the defendant's criminal history, and the presence of any prior DV-related convictions.

Charge Description Typical Bail (LA County)
PC § 273.5(a) Corporal injury to spouse / cohabitant (felony) $50,000 – $100,000
PC § 243(e)(1) Battery against spouse / domestic partner (misdemeanor) $10,000 – $20,000
PC § 273.5 with prior conviction Felony DV with prior DV history $100,000+
PC § 422 Criminal threats (often charged alongside DV) $50,000

Note that bail enhancements can apply if a firearm was involved, if the victim sustained serious bodily injury, or if a child was present during the incident.

How to Post Bail Fast After a DV Arrest in Rowland Heights

Once booking is complete and bail has been set, a bail bond can be posted immediately — there is no mandatory waiting period in California for DV arrests. The fastest path to release is working with a licensed bail bondsman.

Here is how the process works:

  • Call a bondsman 24/7. Bail agents like Angels Bail Bonds are available around the clock because arrests do not keep business hours.
  • Pay a 10% premium. The bail bond premium is 10% of the total bail amount — this is the fee for the bondsman's guarantee to the court. On a $50,000 bail, the premium is $5,000. This fee is set by California law and is non-refundable.
  • Sign the indemnitor agreement. A co-signer (family member, friend) agrees to be responsible if the defendant misses a court date.
  • Bondsman posts the bond. The agent delivers the bond to Industry Sheriff's Station or Twin Towers, and the release process begins.

Release typically takes 2 to 6 hours after the bond is posted, depending on how busy the facility is at the time.

DV-Specific Considerations You Need to Understand

Domestic violence arrests come with conditions and complications that do not apply to most other charges. Before your loved one is released, make sure you understand the following.

Emergency Protective Orders (EPOs). When Sheriff's deputies make a DV arrest, they almost always issue an Emergency Protective Order on the spot. This order is effective immediately and typically lasts 5 to 7 days. It often prohibits the arrested person from returning home or contacting the alleged victim — even if the victim wants contact. Violating an EPO is a separate criminal offense and can cause bail to be revoked.

Criminal Protective Orders at Arraignment. At the arraignment hearing at Deukmejian Courthouse in Pomona, the judge will almost certainly issue a Criminal Protective Order (CPO) as a condition of release. These orders can remain in place for the duration of the case — sometimes years. The defendant must understand the terms before leaving the courthouse.

No mandatory 72-hour hold. Despite a common misconception, California does not impose a mandatory 72-hour hold on DV arrests. Bail can be posted as soon as booking is complete and bail has been set. Do not wait — contact a bondsman right away.

The victim cannot drop the charges. Many families in Rowland Heights are surprised to learn this: once a DV arrest is made, the decision to prosecute rests with the LA County District Attorney's office — not the alleged victim. The victim can express their wishes, but they cannot unilaterally end the case.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does booking take at Industry Sheriff's Station?

Booking typically takes 4 to 8 hours at Industry Station, though it can be longer if the facility is busy or if the person is being transferred to Twin Towers. During this time, you can call Angels Bail Bonds to start the paperwork so we are ready to post the moment booking is complete.

Can bail be posted right away after a DV arrest in Rowland Heights?

Yes. As soon as booking is complete and bail has been set, a bail bond can be posted. There is no mandatory waiting period for domestic violence arrests in California. The sooner you call a bail bondsman, the sooner release can happen.

What is the 10% bail premium and where does that money go?

The 10% premium is the bail bondsman's fee for guaranteeing the full bail amount to the court. On a $50,000 bail, you pay $5,000 to the bondsman. This fee is set by California Department of Insurance regulations and is non-refundable — it is the cost of the bondsman's service, regardless of the outcome of the case.

Can the alleged victim ask for bail to be removed or denied?

The alleged victim does not control the bail process. Bail is set by the court based on the charges, criminal history, and public safety considerations — not by the victim's preferences. However, a victim may speak at an arraignment hearing, and a judge can take their statements into account when deciding whether to modify bail conditions.
