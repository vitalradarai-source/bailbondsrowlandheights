Rowland Heights Cases We Handle Every Week

Below are the charges that come across the 150 N Hudson Ave booking desk most often. Each has a specific Los Angeles County bail schedule entry — we know the number before you finish reading the report.

👪

Domestic Violence (PC 273.5 / 243(e)(1))

Families panic over "the 72-hour rule" that doesn't exist in California DV law. Once LASD Industry Sheriff's Station finishes booking — usually within hours — a surety bond can post on any PC 273.5 or 243(e)(1) charge. Restraining-order terms are handled by the East District LA Superior Court at Pomona on Mission Blvd judge at arraignment, not the jail.

☣

Drug Possession (HS 11377 / 11350)

We see HS 11377 possession and HS 11550 "under the influence" charges weekly from the Industry Hills Expo equestrian grounds or Nogales St corridor. The difference in scheduled bail between those two codes is significant — we don't quote until we've read the booking charge, because the wrong assumption costs hundreds in premium.

🚘

DUI (VC 23152 / 23153)

First-DUI bail in Los Angeles County is $5,000 unless there's an aggravating factor. A priors stack or VC 23153 injury changes the math fast. We pull the charge off the booking sheet and tell you the bail amount before you ask. East District LA Superior Court at Pomona on Mission Blvd handles the arraignment.

⚠

Assault & Battery (PC 240 / 245)

Battery and assault charges from the Industry Hills Expo equestrian grounds or Nogales St corridor area share a booking desk at LASD Industry Sheriff's Station but have very different bail amounts. $20,000 for 242, $50,000 for 245(a)(1). If the booking slip shows a "with great bodily injury" enhancement, the number climbs again.

📑

Felony Bonds up to $500,000

We write bonds up to $500,000 out of LASD Industry Sheriff's Station. For felony cases above $50K, collateral (usually a Hacienda Heights home's equity line) backs the indemnitor agreement. No equity pulled at signing — just held as security until the court case closes.

📋

Warrants, Holds & Immigration Detainers

A probation hold, ICE detainer, or other-county warrant stapled to the booking slip is a stop sign. LASD Industry Sheriff's Station won't release even with bail posted. We read every hold on the report and explain the sequence — what clears when — before quoting.

How a Bail Bond Gets Someone Out of LASD Industry Sheriff's Station

Here's the short version. An arrest at LASD Industry Sheriff's Station, booking, charge filed, bail amount set per Los Angeles County schedule. A bail bondsman (us) writes a surety bond for the full amount and posts it. Your family pays 10% — that's the premium, a one-time fee capped by California Insurance Code § 1800.4.

Your loved one is released pending court. Every hearing at East District LA Superior Court at Pomona on Mission Blvd they attend, the bond stays active. Miss a hearing and the court forfeits the bond to collect the full face value — at which point the indemnitor (the person who signed with us) owes the remainder. Show up to court and none of that ever matters.

Rowland Heights Bail Bond FAQ

What happens if the defendant misses a court date at East District LA Superior Court at Pomona?

The court declares a bail forfeiture. We have roughly 180 days to locate the defendant and bring them back — that's when recovery agents work. If we don't, the bond pays out in full, which is why the indemnitor signed a joint agreement. We call the indemnitor the moment a hearing is missed — almost always something fixable in the first 48 hours.

How much is bail for a first-offense DUI booked at LASD Industry Sheriff's Station?

Standard Los Angeles County scheduled bail for a first-offense VC 23152 is $5,000 — a $500 premium. Injury DUI (VC 23153) jumps to $100,000, a $10,000 premium on which we structure payment plans. The schedule amount holds until the East District LA Superior Court at Pomona on Mission Blvd judge reviews the case at arraignment.

What's the difference between a bail bond and a "bail loan"?

No such product exists in California. A surety bail bond is a contract between Angels Bail Bonds, our insurance underwriter, and East District LA Superior Court at Pomona on Mission Blvd. We guarantee court appearances. The 10% you pay is the premium on that guarantee, capped by CA Insurance Code § 1800.4. Anyone in Rowland Heights advertising "bail loans" is either unlicensed or charging illegal fees. Ask for the license — ours is #1K06080.

Do I need to put up my home as collateral for a Rowland Heights bond?

For most misdemeanors and smaller felonies out of LASD Industry Sheriff's Station, no — the 10% premium plus a signed indemnitor agreement is enough. Collateral typically enters the picture above $50,000 or when there's a documented flight-risk history. When it does, equity in a Rowland Heights or Hacienda Heights home acts as security — not a lien we execute at signing.
